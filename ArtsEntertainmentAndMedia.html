Arts, Entertainment and Media Bios

I am Michael Alcantar, an student and artist interested in the representation of the Filipinx artist in the contemporary art world. Filipinx artists use a variety of mediums to express their concerns about their identities, urban life in the Philippines, issues of xenophobia, displacement, and indigeneity, among other motifs. I focused on ten Philippines based artists working creatively in the contemporary field to bring attention to the growing community of Filpinx artists. I am half Mexican, a quarter Filipino and a quarter European, and much of my own artwork involves reflecting on my identity and structures of society that put us into predetermined social groups. I am interested in Art education to inform young people about the power of art in communities and society in general.

Hi, my name is Kayla Gotangco and I am a senior in the women and gender studies program at San Francisco State University. I am a Filipina-Spanish American. My interests revolve around analyzing a plethora of platforms using learned feminist discourses while also creating my own approach to feminist theory. I am interested in discovering how feminist theory and the health industry intersect. I am currently in the midst of furthering my analysis of Alex Garland’s Ex Machina, in order to critique the inner workings of the film industry as well as offer suggestions for how we might create a more diverse industry in terms of race, gender, and story arcs.
